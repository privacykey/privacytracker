# Code of Conduct

privacytracker is committed to a welcoming, safe, and constructive community.
This applies to issues, pull requests, discussions, reviews, and other spaces
where someone represents the project.

## Expected behavior

- Be respectful, patient, and specific.
- Assume good intent while addressing the actual impact of words and actions.
- Welcome different backgrounds, abilities, viewpoints, and experience levels.
- Give and receive technical feedback without personal attacks.
- Respect privacy and do not publish another person's information without
  permission.

Harassment, discrimination, threats, sexualized conduct, deliberate disruption,
impersonation, and retaliation are not acceptable.

## Reporting and enforcement

Do not report conduct concerns in a public issue. Use GitHub's **Report content**
menu to report privately to repository administrators. If that option is not
available, use [GitHub Support's abuse-reporting process](https://docs.github.com/en/communities/maintaining-your-safety-on-github/reporting-abuse-or-spam).

Maintainers may edit or remove content, close contributions, limit interaction,
or temporarily or permanently restrict participation. Reports will be handled
as privately as possible, with the safety of affected people taking priority.

This policy is informed by the [Contributor Covenant 3.0](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/3/0/)
and the [GitHub Community Guidelines](https://docs.github.com/en/site-policy/github-terms/github-community-guidelines).
